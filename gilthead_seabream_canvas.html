ü´Ä Physiological Responses

Heart Rate Patterns:

  • Ice-slurry induced bradycardia due to hypoxia at low temperatures
  • No significant differences between stunning methods
  • Ikejime maintained higher heart rates than ice-slurry

Temperature Changes:

  • Non-stunned fish showed faster internal temperature decline
  • Likely due to increased blood flow and movement from stress
  • Significant temperature drop observed from minute 3 onwards

Stress Hormones:

  • Highest cortisol in non-stunned ice-slurry group
  • Lowest cortisol in anaesthetized groups
  • Electrical stunning didn't reduce cortisol vs. unstunned fish
ü•© Muscle Quality Impact

pH Effects:

  • Electrical stunning and ice-slurry led to lower muscle pH
  • Anaesthesia resulted in higher pH and better quality
  • Lower pH indicates stress and strong muscle contractions

Texture Properties:

  • Anaesthetized fish: softer, less chewy, less gummy
  • Better water-holding capacity with anaesthesia
  • Lower pH causes protein denaturation and moisture loss
Quality Implication: PCA analysis showed clear separation between anaesthetized and electrically stunned fish in meat quality parameters.

Click to expand: How the study was set up and why it's problematic ‚¨áÔ∏è
In Simple Terms: Imagine trying to test if different medicines work by only giving each medicine to 4-5 people, using different hospitals on different days, and not having a proper way to measure if the medicine actually worked. That's essentially what happened here with fish slaughter methods.
Critical Sample Size Problem: Only 32 fish total across 6 treatment combinations (n = 4-5 per group) severely limits statistical power and robustness.
  • Unbalanced Design: Unequal group sizes (4-5 fish) impact model assumptions and statistical validity (Like comparing test scores from classes with different numbers of students)
  • Tank/Day Confounding: Only 3 tanks used (one per day) prevents separation of treatment effects from environmental variation (Can't tell if differences are from the treatment or just different tank conditions)
  • Pseudoreplication Risk: Same fish used for multiple measures (visual, physiological, quality) creates non-independent data (Like counting the same person's opinion multiple times in a survey)
  • Surgery Timing: Surgeries staggered by tank/day may introduce unaccounted tank-by-day effects (Operations done on different days might have different conditions affecting results)
  • Recovery Period: 7-day bio-logger recovery may still carry residual surgical stress effects (Fish might still be stressed from surgery, affecting how they respond to treatments)
  • No EEG Validation: Consciousness assessed only via visual indicators not validated for seabream (Like checking if someone is asleep by only looking at them, not using brain monitoring)
  • Order Effects: Treatment randomization limited by tank-day confounding (The order of treatments wasn't truly random due to tank limitations)
üìä Statistical Analysis Weaknesses
Click to expand: Why the math behind the results is questionable ‚¨áÔ∏è
In Simple Terms: Statistical analysis is like using the right tools to measure things accurately. Here, it's like trying to measure the height of a building with a broken ruler, removing measurements that don't fit your expectations, and not checking if your measuring method actually works.
  • Underpowered Analysis: 4-5 fish per group only detects large effects, high risk of Type I/II errors (Too few samples to reliably detect real differences - like trying to determine if a coin is fair by only flipping it 5 times)
  • LMM Assumptions: Linear Mixed Models may violate assumptions with small n and limited replication (Using advanced statistical methods that require more data than available)
  • Subjective Outlier Removal: Two heart rate points removed based on AIC improvement lacks rigorous justification (Removing inconvenient data points to make results look better)
  • Inconsistent Statistical Approaches: Mixed use of parametric/non-parametric tests when assumptions violated (Switching between different statistical methods without clear rules)
  • No Visual Indicator Analysis: All consciousness data presented descriptively without inferential statistics (Just describing what they saw without testing if it's statistically meaningful)
  • ANOVA Assumptions: Small, unbalanced groups undermine normality and homogeneity assumptions (Using statistical tests that don't work well with uneven, small groups)
  • Multiple Testing: No correction across multiple outcomes increases false positive risk (Testing many things at once without adjusting for the increased chance of false findings)
  • Missing Effect Sizes: No Cohen's d or eta squared reported limits biological relevance interpretation (Not telling us how big or important the differences actually are in real-world terms)

üí° Main Conclusions
Primary Finding: Slaughter without stunning causes significant welfare concerns and produces inferior meat quality.

Method Rankings (Welfare + Quality):

  1. Anaesthesia + Ikejime: Best welfare and quality (not commercially viable)
  2. Ikejime alone: Good welfare, excellent quality (labor-intensive)
  3. Electrical stunning + Ikejime: Uncertain welfare, good quality
  4. Electrical stunning + Ice-slurry: Uncertain welfare, poor quality
  5. No stunning + Ice-slurry: Poor welfare, poor quality

Critical Need: Development of validated, scalable stunning methods that ensure immediate and sustained unconsciousness while maintaining product quality for commercial aquaculture operations.
